ENTJ

The Field Marshal

Overview

ENTJ: Extroversion, Intuition, Thinking, Judging. ENTJs are great leaders and decision makers. They are brilliant thinkers and long-term planners. Being very practical and logical, they excel at tasks requiring reasoning and intelligence. ENTJs value truth and will only be convinced through logical reasoning. ENTJs are very systematic in planning and researching new things. They are natural, honest and enthusiastic leaders who are good at organizing people. They live very disciplined lives and expect others to do the same. Therefore, they challenge and push others to work as hard as they do.

Detailed Characteristics

Friendly, strong-willed, and direct; honest and rational, with high standards for themselves and others; constantly strive to demonstrate their abilities; have a global perspective; capable and decisive, with a well-organized approach; and enjoy working with others.

For ENTJs, the most important thing in life is to showcase their strengths and achieve significant accomplishments. They are natural leaders and commanders. This is evident not only in their charm and leadership skills when they are world leaders but also in their leadership skills when they are religious leaders.

ENTJs often describe their projects and plans in a narrative style, which is useful for salespeople, comedians, and storytellers. Their natural ability to "block" others gives them an edge in convincing customers to accept their requests.

ENTJs are decisive. They know exactly what needs to be done and then assign the tasks accordingly. Other types of people rarely have the same ability to make quick decisions and defeat strong opponents when faced with challenges. When challenged, they become very argumentative; or they may give a cold stare, which seems to convey the message: ENTJ cannot be underestimated.

Weaknesses

ENTJs sometimes rush to make decisions. Taking a moment to slow down can give them a chance to gather all relevant data and consider the situation carefully. However, ENTJs are generally straightforward and impatient, insensitive, inflexible, and difficult to approach. They need to listen to the voices of those around them and appreciate their contributions. They often focus too much on reality and neglect feelings. When their feelings are ignored or not expressed, they are very sensitive. If someone questions their abilities, especially someone they respect, this feeling is particularly strong. They may lose their temper over small things and this outburst can hurt those close to them. If they take some time to reflect and understand their true feelings, they will be very happy and the effect will be good. Correctly releasing their emotions instead of exploding will help them better control themselves and achieve their desired and hard-fought positions.

Work Advantages

As an ENTJ, your job satisfaction comes from doing these jobs:

1. Lead, control, organize, and improve the operation of an institution so that it can run effectively and achieve its goals.

2. Allow me to engage in long-term planning, creative problem-solving, and designing logical solutions for various issues.

3. My work is completed in a structured environment, and I and my colleagues work under a series of clear guidelines.

4. My work encourages and stimulates my curiosity, allowing me to tackle complex and often difficult issues.

5. Give me the opportunity to interact with and work with various people, who must be capable, interesting, and powerful.

6. Give me opportunities to advance, improve, and showcase my abilities within the organization.

7. My work is challenging, competitive, and competitive, and I am the center of attention in the workplace. My achievements are visible, recognized, and can be rewarded.

8. Allow me to work with talented, creative, ambitious, and idealistic people, and their abilities are something I admire.

9. Allow me to set goals and strive for them, allowing me to use my organizational skills to focus everyone's efforts on a larger goal while ensuring that all my goals are achieved on time and effectively.

10. Allow me to manage and supervise others using logic, objective standards, and utilizing everyone's talents, without dealing with interpersonal conflicts.

The differences between different personality types can sometimes be subtle, and sometimes very obvious. Extroverts love "interaction" and we encourage them to do so boldly; however, introverts only like to "interact" with a small group of people they know, and they find it easier to interact with familiar people.
