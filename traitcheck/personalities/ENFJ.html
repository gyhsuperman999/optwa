ENFJ

The Teacher

Overview

ENFJ is one of the MBTI personality test results. ENFJ stands for: Extroversion (E) + Intuition (N) + Feeling (F) + Judging (J). ENFJs are providers who are naturally inclined to serve others, ensuring they have a sense of material abundance and group belonging. They consciously take on the responsibility of caring for those around them and arranging their welfare. They are also the most socially active among the guardian types. Wherever they go, they take on the role of social contributors, happily spending their time and energy to ensure others' needs are met, traditions are maintained and developed, and social responsibilities are fulfilled completely.

Type Analysis

Due to their kind and gentle nature, they prefer to take on the role of guardian rather than protector. Being extroverted and graceful, they seem to prefer being providers rather than protectors. They make up 2-5% of the population, with 95% being female, which is fortunate for us because cooperation and social convenience are key elements of their human nature. They excel at working collaboratively with their assistants and tirelessly attend to all the details of goods and service arrangements.

These expressive guardians are graceful, talkative, and become uneasy when isolated. Even in a group of strangers, they can start a conversation and chat pleasantly. They show a strong interest in small talk about everything. They are more accustomed to thinking about visible rather than fictional things, easily recognizing and discussing major events or difficulties in people's lives. They struggle with abstract topics but tend to listen to recognized authorities speak about abstract things, and often rely on officially approved views as their standard for judging things.

Type Description

The most important thing for ENFJs is the opportunity to interact with others and to have the chance to lead. ENFJs have strong leadership qualities and the ability to manipulate others. This ability mainly comes from their excellent interpersonal skills and sales skills. ENFJs deeply believe in their dreams and see themselves as saviors and capable people, and they are often right.

ENFJs are versatile learners and have a broad perspective. Some can quickly start multiple amazing projects at the same time. Many ENFJs have strong entrepreneurial abilities.

When describing ENFJs, we often think of organizational and decision-making skills. However, they are not like SJ or NTJ people who can organize the environment or sudden crises. They are good at organizing interpersonal events. Their offices are either clean or messy, but they can summarize people's motivations faster and more flexibly than JFP people.

ENFJs understand and appreciate others. Like most NF types, they focus on others' needs rather than their own. They have fewer psychological barriers, so they are often subjected to unfair treatment or criticism. Therefore, ENFJs are under more pressure than others.

Friendly, polite, and caring; they are emotionally invested and can be easily hurt; they are creative and decisive; they are responsible and have a plan; they are good at communicating and can lead discussions.

Type Characteristics

ENFJs love people. They value human relationships more than anything else. They naturally care about others. They love life and feel connected to everything.

Because they are idealists and value their own worth, ENFJs are loyal to their respected people, careers, and work units. They have energy and enthusiasm, as well as responsibility and prudence, and perseverance.

ENFJs have a natural tendency to criticize themselves. However, because they believe they have a responsibility for others' feelings, ENFJs rarely criticize others in public. They are very clear about what is (or is not) appreciated behavior and are very friendly, charming, and sophisticated. They are natural leaders and are popular and charismatic. They are good at communicating and can use their eloquence.

ENFJs make decisions based on their feelings rather than facts. They are interested in the possibilities of the future and the impact of these possibilities on others.

They have a natural sense of order and expect the same from others. They like to get things done and sometimes make decisions for others.

ENFJs are empathetic and can understand, support, and help others. They are responsible and serious. Because they are idealists, they always see the good side of others.

Weaknesses

ENFJs can become overly serious and emotional, which can lead them to over-involve themselves in others' problems or emotions. Sometimes they choose careers that are not worth the time and effort they put into them. When things don't go as planned, they feel disappointed or hopeless. This can make them retreat and feel unappreciated.

Due to their strong desire for harmony, ENFJs may ignore their own needs and overlook practical issues. Because they always avoid conflicts, they sometimes maintain an unfair and dishonest relationship. ENFJs are overly concerned about others' feelings, which can lead them to ignore important facts that may lead to criticism or hurt feelings.

ENFJs need to learn to accept and handle conflicts, as this is also an important part of interpersonal relationships.

ENFJs are overly emotional and can overlook the actual consequences of their actions. Pay attention to facts rather than people to help them make more reasonable decisions.

ENFJs are very sensitive to criticism and can react very badly to it. They find it difficult to accept harmless and well-intentioned criticism and usually react in a panic, hurt, or angry way. Their behavior can become very irrational, even to the point of losing their rationality. ENFJs should take a step back and objectively assess the situation before making decisions.

ENFJs are very idealistic and believe that the world is what they want or hope it to be. They believe in ideal human relationships and ignore facts that contradict their beliefs. People who haven't learned to face reality will eventually ignore problems instead of trying to solve them. In short, ENFJs need to be more realistic.
