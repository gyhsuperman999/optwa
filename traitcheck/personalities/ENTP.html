ENTP

The Inventor

Overview

For ENTP types, the most important thing is to be creative, envision a beautiful future, and always maintain a sense of challenge. "Smart" is the most appropriate word to describe this type of person. ENTPs speak and think quickly. They enjoy debating both for their own benefit and to show off their debating skills. During debates, they like to display quirky humor and play devil's advocate. Those who don't view debate as a form of display often feel confused and even hurt when arguing with them.

Detailed Characteristics

Because ENTPs are very articulate, they are clever and skillful in problem-solving. However, sometimes they strive to "surpass" themselves, often "using any means necessary" - as long as conditions allow, they ignore certain rules to take shortcuts, even if it means failing in ambitious deceptive activities. Whether at work or at home, they like to play with various physical or intellectual toys, the more complex the better. Although they quickly become bored with old things.

ENTPs are basically optimistic. When facing major setbacks, they view them only as challenges and determinedly solve them; however, when encountering small setbacks or troubles, they easily lose their temper. ENTPs cannot tolerate those who are stubborn and slow-witted, and they make no effort to hide this. Actually, as long as there's nothing troublesome in their lives, they are usually friendly and pleasant, sometimes even quite likable.

Interpersonal Relationships

In terms of interpersonal relationships, they can quickly become intimate with those they love. Some people express these feelings privately; while others display their intimacy with loved ones so publicly that it surprises coworkers who only see their work side. ENTPs are also good at making friends with people who are as smart and interesting as they are. Apart from these two aspects, other aspects of human nature seem to be forgotten by ENTPs, unless when they are listeners - giving people either good, bad, or mixed impressions.

Basic Description

Friendly and lovable, frank and outspoken; quick-witted, energetic, cynical; imaginative and innovative; curious and flexible, unpredictable; logical and analytical

When situations and environments change, you easily ignore others' feelings, becoming insensitive and rash.

Advantages

Excellent social skills, able to make others excited about their views; eager to "think outside the box," can come up with new possibilities; outstanding creative problem-solving skills, adventurous spirit, creativity, and courage to overcome difficulties; independent, able to work alone and concentrate; able to comprehensively consider and apply large amounts of information; rational curiosity when collecting needed information; able to analyze things logically even under great pressure; confident in learning new knowledge and skills; able to grasp the big picture, understand the long-term impact of thoughts and behaviors; ability to adapt flexibly to new situations, skilled at improvisation; not feeling constrained in social situations, able to comfortably adapt to most social occasions

Disadvantages

Difficult to be orderly and organized; often unable to prioritize tasks; overconfident, may inappropriately use their abilities and social experiences; tend to view things in terms of "is it possible" rather than measuring the probability; likely to make unrealistic promises; lack patience with narrow-minded and stubborn people; once a problem is solved, interest disappears; don't like following traditional, formulaic methods; may lack patience for details and follow-up work; easily bored with things and may shift attention at inappropriate times; don't like repetitive work; lack patience with people they don't trust

Suitable Positions

Opportunities for creative problem-solving work, work with logical order and fair standards, ability to increase personal power and frequently communicate with powerful people. For example, various project planners and initiators, investment advisors (real estate, finance, trade, business, etc.), business owners, association leaders, government officials, etc.; investment brokers, financial planners, art directors, talk show hosts, public relations professionals, corporate public relations managers, etc.; product development, marketing planning, management system development, industrial design managers, investment industry employees, advertising creative directors, etc.

Suitable Fields

Investment, banking, public relations, politics, marketing, entrepreneurship, creative fields.

Love

ENTP personality types are naturally good-natured, open-minded, and carefree. Being with them always feels very happy. They gain pleasure and satisfaction through interaction with others. They are particularly interested in discussing theories and concepts. Because they enjoy the process of arguing with others, they might become the spark for controversy. They generally like teasing and group activities, and can appear charming in such social interactions. Their problem is that when they're looking for new ideas or plans, they sometimes neglect their close ones.

Advantages in Relationships

Passionate, positive and upbeat, popular, with strong personal charisma, excellent communication skills, particularly focused on improving and cultivating their abilities during interactions, relaxed and good at self-adjustment, usually very easy to get along with, wise people who can always come up with great strategies or ideas, very good at making money but not good at financial management, very serious about their commitments and interpersonal relationships, able to quickly recover from emotional setbacks

Disadvantages in Relationships

Always excited about new things, they might frequently change their lovers or friends, often abandon the execution of their plans or ideas, their love of debate might cause conflicts with others, love adventure and spend money without restraint, usually not good at financial management, although they value their lovers and friends highly, if the relationship doesn't help them grow, they might choose to leave

ENTP as Lovers

In intimate relationships, ENTPs pursue similar goals as they do in ordinary friendships - "progress and growth." They constantly ask themselves questions like "How can we improve the quality of our relationship?" "Where is this relationship heading?" "Am I growing in this relationship?" They are always enthusiastic about accepting new ideas, plans, and partners that they believe can help their progress and knowledge. Generally speaking, ENTPs' enthusiasm and intentions in a relationship are positive.

One major problem with ENTPs is that they often can't persist in executing their plans. This can be troublesome for their partners. As problems escalate, their partners gradually lose confidence in ENTPs, not believing they can match words with actions. Eventually, in romantic relationships, ENTPs become like the boy who cried wolf.

Regarding sex, ENTPs are passionate and put great effort into continuously improving both parties' experience. They are fully invested and questioning. They like to be natural while following rules, and are open to new concepts and adventures. They view sex more as a physical expression of conveying love rather than a direct opportunity to express love.

Overall, ENTPs have a childlike enthusiasm and genuine interest in the health and direction of love. Therefore, they hope to create a healthy growing romantic relationship with their partner. ENTPs need to pay attention to their feelings. When faced with exciting external potential values, ENTPs cannot ignore their emotions.

While any two well-developed individuals can have a healthy relationship, the best matches for ENTPs are INFJ or INTJ. ENTPs' dominant function is extroverted intuition, which best matches with those whose dominant function is introverted intuition. Although any two individuals of any personality type can have a healthy relationship if they are well-developed, the natural partners most suitable for ENTPs are INFJ or INTJ types. ENTPs' dominant function "extroverted intuition" matches best with these two personality types' dominant function "introverted intuition".
